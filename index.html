Context

Why This Program Exists

Equitable workforce outcomes require more than good programs, they require well-prepared leaders.

The OA Fellows Curriculum was designed to address a specific gap: workforce professionals who understand their communities deeply but need structured support to lead systems-level change.

Workforce systems across the country are navigating real complexity — shifting labor markets, rising skill demands, and persistent gaps in who benefits from economic growth. Addressing these conditions requires leaders who can think beyond individual programs.

Within the Goodwill network, organizations implementing the Opportunity Accelerator framework need professionals equipped to lead with fidelity, design with equity, and execute with the discipline that systemic change demands.

That leadership capacity does not develop on its own. It must be intentionally built. The OA Fellows Curriculum is that vehicle — an 18-week cohort experience moving fellows from understanding to action, from theory to applied practice, from individual learning to network-wide impact.

Program Goals

What the Program Sets Out to Achieve

A
Develop Systems-Level Leadership
Equip workforce professionals with the frameworks, language, and analytical skills to understand economic mobility as a systems challenge — and to respond with strategies that are equitable, evidence-based, and community-rooted.
B
Build Organizational Implementation Capacity
Prepare fellows to implement the OA framework with fidelity inside their organizations — applying diagnostic tools, designing human-centered programs, and driving quality improvement through evidence and accountability.
C
Strengthen Network Cohesion
Build a cohort of leaders across Goodwill Industries International who share a common framework and standard for equitable workforce practice — enabling coordination, peer learning, and collective impact across the network.
D
Connect Learning to Real Organizational Impact
Ensure every competency fellows develop is applied — not just studied. Through progressive skill-building and a capstone project, fellows deliver tangible value back to their organizations as part of the program itself.
Curriculum Architecture

Three Phases of the OA Framework

The curriculum is organized around the three phases of the Opportunity Accelerator model, each building on the last — moving fellows from understanding to application to synthesis.

Behavioral Assessment

Predictive Index
Assessment & Team Formation

All OA Fellows complete the Predictive Index (PI) Behavioral Assessment — a six-minute, research-backed instrument that measures four core behavioral drives. Results are used to intentionally compose capstone groups so that every team reflects a diversity of strengths, tendencies, and working styles — mirroring the collaborative demands of real workforce systems change.

Beyond team formation, PI insights help fellows understand their own leadership style, how they show up for others, and where they may need to flex their approach to achieve better outcomes.

How PI Powers Capstone Group Formation

01
Fellows Complete the Assessment
Each fellow takes the six-minute PI Behavioral Assessment, measuring natural behavioral drives and workplace tendencies.
02
Workshop A — "Who Am I?"
Fellows unpack their individual profiles in a virtual workshop, exploring strengths, caution areas, and leadership style. Personal development action plans are created.
03
Capstone Groups Are Assigned
PI data is used to intentionally compose capstone teams — ensuring each group brings a range of behavioral drives and roles to tackle complex organizational challenges.
Informed by PI Results
04
Workshop B — "Building Effective Teams"
In-person, fellows use PI Team Discovery to map their collective tendencies, identify team strengths and caution areas, and build action plans to accelerate capstone execution.

The Four Behavioral Drives

PI measures these four dimensions to create a complete picture of how a person naturally works, leads, and collaborates in workplace settings.

D
Dominance
Drive to exert influence, take control, and achieve results independently.
E
Extraversion
Drive to connect with others, build relationships, and gain social approval.
P
Patience
Drive for consistency, stability, and a deliberate, methodical pace.
F
Formality
Drive to conform to rules, structure, and high standards of quality.
Why It Matters
Diverse Teams, Stronger Capstones
By using PI data to form capstone groups, the OA Fellows program ensures every team includes people who naturally fill different roles — from strategic visionaries to detail-oriented executors — producing more rigorous, balanced, and effective solutions.
The Book Behind the Curriculum

Growing Fairly

The curriculum is grounded in Growing Fairly: How to Build Opportunity and Equity in Workforce Development — a 2022 Brookings Institution Press book by Stephen Goldsmith and Kate Markin Coleman. Written by practitioners for practitioners, it sets out ten principles for designing a more effective and equitable workforce system. Eight chapters are integrated across the 18 weeks as active design lenses, not background reading.

About the Book

The U.S. labor market faces a contradictory challenge: employers struggle to find qualified applicants while millions remain out of work or underemployed. Growing Fairly offers reforms that meet the needs of both workers and employers — arguing for skill-based hiring, distributed performance data, and cross-sector collaboration. Its take is optimistic. Written by practitioners for practitioners.

Authors

Stephen Goldsmith — Derek Bok Professor at Harvard's Kennedy School of Government; former mayor of Indianapolis and deputy mayor of New York City.

Kate Markin Coleman — Principal of IAS Advising, LLC; former EVP and Chief Strategy Officer of YMCA of the USA.

How It's Used

Each chapter is integrated as a design lens — asking fellows to apply the book's evidence and frameworks directly to their own organizations and communities. The book moves from theory to tool across all 18 weeks.
